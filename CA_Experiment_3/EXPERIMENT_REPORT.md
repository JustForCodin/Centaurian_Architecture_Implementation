## 1. Objective

The Centaurian Architecture (CA) v3 paper claims a behavioural advantage for the **Quantum Personality Model (QPM)** — an 11-qubit + 1-ancilla circuit that represents the Big Five aspect-level traits as a density operator under Lindblad-discretised noise — over any classical alternative. Three specific behavioural claims are made:

1. **Non-commutative order effects** — re-ordering the same situative inputs produces measurably different downstream states.
2. **Genuine ambivalence under conflict** — superposition lets the model hold opposing trait poles simultaneously, where a classical Gaussian would average them to a midpoint.
3. **Higher downstream output quality** — when the QPM personality state is translated into structured intent for the SLM transducer, the resulting utterances are scored higher by the PersonaScore rubric than utterances generated from a matched classical baseline.

Experiment 3 tests all three claims against a deliberately strong classical control: **CMG-CDK** (Correlated Multivariate Gaussian with Context-Dependent Kernel) — a model matched to QPM on every dimension *except* the quantum-specific mechanisms (superposition, non-commutative evolution, coherence). Any measured difference between QPM and CMG-CDK outputs is therefore attributable specifically to quantum-like mechanisms rather than parameter count, correlation structure, or context sensitivity.

### 1.1 Pre-registered Hypotheses

| ID | Hypothesis | Pass criterion |
|----|-----------|----------------|
| **H1** | QPM produces larger order-effect asymmetry than CMG-CDK under reversed situative input sequences. | Paired t-test p < 0.05 on JSD per pair, QPM > CMG |
| **H2** | Under conflicting situative inputs, QPM output distributions have higher Shannon entropy than CMG-CDK outputs. | Paired t-test p < 0.05 on H per scenario, QPM > CMG |
| **H3** | Utterances generated by the SLM from QPM personality states score higher on PersonaScore than utterances generated from CMG-CDK states, holding situative context and knowledge triples constant. | Paired t-test p < 0.05 on PersonaScore per (script, turn, dim), QPM > CMG |
| **H4** | QPM stochastic variance across repeated runs is small relative to the QPM−CMG signal — i.e. shot noise does not dominate the comparison. | SNR = ‖μ_QPM − μ_CMG‖₂ / σ_within_QPM ≥ 3.0 |

The decision rule (plan §8.5) explicitly maps every combination of (H1, H2, H3) outcomes to a paper-update prescription, pre-committing to the action *before* observing results.

## 2. Method

### 2.1 Experimental Design

Four parallel batteries run on identical inputs through both QPM and CMG-CDK. Battery A tests order effects (H1) at the personality-state level. Battery B tests ambivalence under conflict (H2). Battery C tests downstream PersonaScore via the SLM transducer (H3). The H4 variance-calibration run confirms shot noise does not dominate the signal.

| Battery | Hypothesis | Inputs | Models | Output metric |
|---------|-----------|--------|--------|----------------|
| **A** | H1 | 30 sequence pairs × 2 orderings (AB / BA), 5 categories | QPM (1024 shots) and CMG-CDK (1024 samples) | Jensen-Shannon Divergence over aligned distributions |
| **B** | H2 | 20 conflict scenarios designed to push opposing trait poles | Same | Mean Bernoulli entropy across 11 trait dimensions, derived from marginals |
| **C** | H3 | 30 scripts (22 natural + 8 adversarial) × 40 turns × 8 probe turns × 4 dimensions = 960 paired probes per profile | Same, driving Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct + LoRA-10K via QPM-to-structured-intent translation | PersonaScore 1–5 from Sonnet 4.5 judge |
| **H4** | H4 | 1 fixed d-vector × 10 QPM repeats | QPM only (×10) + one CMG-CDK reference | SNR = ‖μ_QPM − μ_CMG‖₂ / σ_within_QPM |

Both personality profiles (`psychotherapy` and `software_eng` from Table 33 of the v3 paper) were run on all three main batteries; H4 was run on `psychotherapy` only. `psychotherapy` is the primary profile because the script bank (carried over from Experiments 1 and 2) is in the psychotherapy domain and the SLM system prompt anchors Aria as a psychotherapy support agent; `software_eng` serves as a robustness check for H1/H2 by varying the initial s_k initialisation.

### 2.2 QPM Configuration

Qiskit Aer 12-qubit circuit (11 trait + 1 ancilla):

| Stage | Gates | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 — Ry initialisation | 11 Ry, θ = 2·arcsin(√s_k) | Per-aspect Big-Five initial state from profile s_k values |
| 2 — Intra-domain CNOT | 5 CX | Within-domain trait correlations (DeYoung et al. 2007) |
| 3 — Inter-domain CRz | 8 CRz, φ = arcsin(ρ)·π | Cross-domain correlations (van der Linden et al. 2010 Table 6), X-CRz-X for ρ<0 |
| 4 — Context Ry (×N turns) | 10 Ry per turn, θ = δ·d_j (δ=0.3) | Non-commutative context modulation from 5-dim situative d-vector |
| 5 — Lindblad noise | Per-qubit amplitude + phase damping (composed, Table 4 of v3 paper); μ·d5 depolarising on ancilla | Discretised decoherence |
| 6 — Measurement | 11 measurements (trait qubits only, ancilla traced out) | 1024-shot readout → marginals + purity proxy |

Multi-step d-vector sequences (Battery A) apply Ry context rotations *successively* on the same circuit, so the AB ordering and BA ordering produce different unitary evolutions whenever the intervening state has any superposition or entanglement.

### 2.3 CMG-CDK Configuration (Classical Control)

`cmg_cdk.py` implements a deliberately matched classical baseline:

| Dimension | QPM | CMG-CDK | Match? |
|---|---|---|:-:|
| Trait correlation structure | Encoded in CRz angles φ = arcsin(ρ)·π | Same ρ matrix → 11×11 covariance Σ (Higham-corrected to nearest PD if needed) | ✓ |
| Context sensitivity | Ry(δ·d_j) per coupling, δ=0.3 | μ ← clip(μ + W·d, 0, 1), W[k, j] = δ for the same (qubit, d_idx) couplings | ✓ |
| Personality initialisation | Ry(2·arcsin(√s_k)) | μ₀ = s_k (same 11-element vector) | ✓ |
| Number of samples | 1024 shots | 1024 multivariate-Gaussian samples (Σ scaled to 0.01 to match QPM measurement noise regime) | ✓ |
| Superposition / coherence | Ry produces |ψ⟩ = α|0⟩ + β|1⟩ | Not present — μ is a real vector | **✗ by design** |
| Order non-commutativity | Ry(θ_A)·Ry(θ_B) ≠ Ry(θ_B)·Ry(θ_A) on entangled state | μ + W·d_A + W·d_B = μ + W·d_B + W·d_A (commutative) | **✗ by design** |

Identical purity proxy formula `1 − mean_k[p̂_k² + (1−p̂_k)²]` (computed from the same marginals) makes the H2 entropy and coherence diagnostics directly comparable. Identical Bernoulli-entropy formula on marginals (`-mean_k[p·ln(p) + (1−p)·ln(1−p)]`) is used for both — eliminating the histogram-degeneracy that would otherwise arise from QPM's binary shot outcomes vs. CMG-CDK's continuous Gaussian samples.

### 2.4 Battery C SLM Stack

Identical to Experiment 2 Condition C, with the personality JSON field overridden per turn from QPM or CMG-CDK marginals:

- **Subject model:** Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct + LoRA-10K adapter (the headline adapter from Experiment 2), 4-bit NF4 quantisation.
- **SCI:** Combined SCI (refresh injection at turns 15 and 30, episodic-RAG on E-probes, compressed events in system prompt). System prompt is locked to "Aria, professional AI support agent specialising in psychotherapy support" — only the `personality` field varies between conditions.
- **Probe schedule:** identical RNG seed schema to Exp 1/2 — 4 dimensions × 8 probe turns × 30 scripts = 960 paired observations per profile.
- **Judge:** Claude Sonnet 4.5, same rubric as Exp 1/2.

The single experimental variable is therefore the personality marginals (11 floating-point values) injected per turn into the JSON self-model.

### 2.5 D-vector Extraction

A lightweight VADER + keyword pipeline (`extract_d_vector` in `ca_assets.py`) maps each user message in Battery C to a 5-dim situative vector:

- **d1** Affective Intensity — VADER `|compound|`
- **d2** Task Orientation — task-keyword density
- **d3** Social Normative Constraint — long-word ratio (formality proxy)
- **d4** Ambiguity Level — hedge phrases + question marks
- **d5** Temporal Pressure — urgency keyword match

Both QPM and CMG-CDK receive the same d-vector per turn, ensuring the only between-model variable is the personality model.

### 2.6 Evaluation Pipeline

`experiment_runner.py` dispatches batteries via `--battery {A,B,C,H4}`; `analyse_results.py` produces all per-hypothesis paired t-tests, Cohen's d, per-category breakdowns, time-series plots, and the auto-generated decision-rule summary. All runs are resumable — completed pairs/scenarios/scripts are skipped on re-start.

## 4. Methodology Notes

### 4.1 Bernoulli Entropy from Marginals (Both Models)

The original Battery B entropy implementation histogrammed raw 1024-shot QPM outputs and 1024-sample CMG-CDK outputs over a fixed bin grid. This produced a degenerate comparison:

- QPM shot outcomes are binary (0/1 per qubit), so the 11-bit bitstring histogram is sharply concentrated on a handful of bins → low entropy.
- CMG-CDK Gaussian samples spread continuously, populating many more bins at low density → higher entropy.

The bug produced H_CMG ≥ H_QPM — exactly the opposite of the hypothesised direction. Both models now compute entropy as the **mean Bernoulli entropy across trait dimensions**, derived from the same marginals dict:

```python
H = -mean_k[ p̂_k · ln(p̂_k) + (1−p̂_k) · ln(1−p̂_k) ]   ∈ [0, ln 2]
```

This is a one-step diagnostic over the same 11 marginals both models export, so it is directly comparable across architectures and avoids the histogram-discretisation artefact.

### 4.2 Re-exports from `CA_Experiment_2/ca_assets.py`

To keep Battery C byte-identical to Experiment 2 on all non-personality variables, `CA_Experiment_3/ca_assets.py` loads Experiment 2's `ca_assets.py` by file path via `importlib.util.spec_from_file_location` under a different module name (`ca_assets_exp2`) — avoiding a `sys.modules` collision with the Exp-3 module of the same name. It then re-exports the SCI constants used by Battery C: `PROBE_POOL`, `PROBE_TURNS`, `DIMENSIONS`, `RUBRICS`, `JUDGE_SYSTEM_PROMPT`, `PERSONA_JSON`, `PERSONA_JSON_STR`, `EPISODIC_MEMORIES_STR`, `SCI_REFRESH_USER`, `SCI_REFRESH_ASSISTANT`. The last two were missing from the initial implementation and caused an `AttributeError` at turn 15 of script 001 on the first Battery C run; both are now re-exported.

### 4.3 Per-5-turns Progress Prints (Matches Exp 1/2)

Initial Battery C output emitted only the script-header line, the two SCI refresh markers, and a per-model "done" line — leaving 10–15 minutes of silence between visible progress markers on a 30-script × 2-model run. Per-turn progress prints were added to match the Exp 1/2 cadence:

```
T<turn> <model> | ctx:<pct>% | turns=N probes=N | elapsed=X.Ymin
```

This required adding a lightweight `count_tokens_approx(text) = max(1, len(text)//4)` helper (same formula as Exp 1/2 — exactness not needed) and `completed_turns` / `completed_probes` counters per (script, model). The context-fill percentage now also appears in the per-turn JSONL log.

### 4.4 Noise-Model Composition (No Duplicate Warnings)

Qiskit Aer's `NoiseModel.add_quantum_error` warns when two errors are attached to the same (gate, qubit). The initial implementation added amplitude-damping and phase-damping errors separately, producing one warning per trait qubit on every circuit build. Both channels are now `.compose()`d into a single `QuantumError` before being added, suppressing the warning and matching Qiskit's canonical composition pattern.

### 4.5 Qiskit Matplotlib Drawer Display

`circuit.draw('mpl', ...)` and `qiskit.visualization.plot_histogram(...)` both return matplotlib `Figure` objects that are **not** registered with pyplot's figure manager. Calling `plt.show()` on them displays an empty `plt.gcf()` instead of the real figure. The notebook visualisation cells (2b, 2c) use `IPython.display.display(fig)` directly, plus `plt.close(fig)` to prevent double-render. `pylatexenc` is included in Cell 2's pip install — it's required by Qiskit's MPL drawer but not by Qiskit itself.

### 4.6 Software-Engineering Profile Caveat

The `software_eng` Battery C run uses the same psychotherapy script bank and the same hardcoded "Aria, psychotherapy support agent" system prompt — only the `personality` JSON field is overridden by the `software_eng` profile's s_k values. This is **not** a fair cross-domain test (there is no SE-flavoured script bank in the repo); it is a robustness check on H1/H2 against profile variation. Battery C software_eng results were collected but not formally analysed via `analyse_results.py` — the H3 decision is set by the psychotherapy primary, per pre-registration.

### 4.7 Pylatexenc + bitsandbytes Deprecation Warning

Battery C emits a `FutureWarning` from `bitsandbytes/backends/cuda/ops.py:468` (`torch._check_is_size(blocksize)`) on the first inference call of every subprocess. This is a library-side deprecation of an internal PyTorch helper and does not affect results. The warning fires once per Python process; it is visible in Cell 7b because Cell 7's already-cached CUDA kernel state lets Cell 7b take the steady-state fast path that contains this call from the first inference onward.

## 5. Interpretation

### 5.1 Decision Rule Outcome: Internal-State Advantage Confirmed, Behavioural Advantage Not Detected

The pre-registered Decision Rule §8.5 outcome (H1✓ H2✓ H3✗) was the second of the five possible branches, and is reproduced verbatim here:

> *"Quantum advantage exists internally but doesn't reach behavior. Add scope note: 'quantum-like advantage confirmed at internal state level; downstream behavioral advantage not detected at current sample size'"*

The CA v3 paper edits prescribed by this outcome:

- **§2.3 (claims):** Add scope note distinguishing internal-state advantage (H1, H2 confirmed with large effect sizes) from downstream-behavioural advantage (H3 not detected at n = 960 paired probes).
- **§15.4.3 (or new sub-section):** Add Experiment 3 as the empirical grounding for H1 and H2. The order-effect and ambivalence claims now have direct experimental support, citing Cohen's d = 21.51 and d = 2.59 respectively against the matched classical baseline.
- **Table 41 (ablation summary):** Update to reflect that QPM internal-state mechanisms have been ablated against CMG-CDK and confirmed at the internal-state level.
- **§18 (conclusions):** Add sentence: "Experiment 3 empirically confirmed QPM's quantum-like advantages over a matched classical baseline at the internal-state level (order effects, ambivalence) but did not detect a downstream behavioural advantage on PersonaScore at n = 960 paired probes."

The downstream-behavioural advantage is not refuted by this experiment — it is **not detected at current sample size**, which is a more limited claim. The 95% CI on the per-probe Δ includes zero ([−0.024, +0.072]) and the point estimate is +0.024 with d_z = 0.032. A sample-size calculation for detecting d_z = 0.05 at 80% power would require n ≈ 3,150 paired probes; for d_z = 0.10, n ≈ 785. The current n = 960 is therefore appropriately powered to detect d_z ≥ ~0.09, and the observed effect is below that detection threshold.

### 5.2 Why Internal-State Advantage Doesn't Propagate to Behaviour

The most parsimonious diagnosis: the QPM→structured-intent translation (`qpm_to_structured_intent` in `ca_assets.py`) compresses the 11-dim trait state into a `personality` JSON field embedded inside a larger persona prompt. The LLM's downstream behaviour is dominated by the rest of that prompt — Aria's role definition, scenario history, episodic events, register and style constraints — and the subtle trait-value differences QPM produces (typically a few hundredths of a unit per aspect, compared to integer-magnitude swings in the rest of the persona JSON) are below the LLM's response-discrimination threshold.

In quantum-information terms, the QPM → JSON projection is a **measurement-like collapse** that loses the coherence and order-effect signatures the internal QPM state carries. The 11 marginals delivered to the JSON field are the *diagonal* of the density matrix, with all off-diagonal coherence terms discarded. The CMG-CDK matches QPM on the diagonal almost exactly (the H4 calibration's between-model L² = 0.847 is across the *entire* trait vector — for a single turn the per-aspect difference is typically < 0.05); when only the diagonal is exposed downstream, the two models produce nearly indistinguishable inputs to the LLM.

This is a **specification of where the QPM mechanism matters and where it doesn't**, not a refutation of the formalism. The H1/H2 results confirm the QPM produces genuinely different *internal* personality dynamics; the H3 result clarifies that the current SLM-prompting interface does not transmit that difference to the SLM. A richer QPM→behaviour interface (e.g. one that conditions speech-act selection on coherence/purity, or one that uses the full QPM density matrix to steer logits directly rather than via a JSON intermediate) is the natural next experiment.

### 5.3 Effect-Size Asymmetry Across Hypotheses

| Hypothesis | Cohen's d | Conventional interpretation |
|---|---:|---|
| H1 (order effects) | **21.51** | Off-scale. ~30× Cohen's "large" (0.8) threshold |
| H2 (ambivalence) | **2.59** | Off-scale. ~3× "large" |
| H3 (PersonaScore) | 0.03 | Trivial. ~30× *below* "small" (0.2) |

The H1 effect is **two orders of magnitude larger** than the H3 effect. Inside the CA program this asymmetry is informative: it suggests the QPM produces structural differences in personality-state dynamics that are unambiguous at the formalism level (any classical model with a commutative update will fail H1 by definition), but those structural differences are filtered out by the QPM→JSON interface used to drive the SLM. The CA v3 paper's claim of a *behavioural* advantage is therefore stronger than this experiment can support; the *internal-state* advantage is stronger than this experiment needed to establish.

### 5.4 The Sub-Dimension Pattern in H3

The per-dimension breakdown shows the small QPM advantage (Δ = +0.024) is concentrated in the Capability dimension (+0.059), with Trait, Episodic, and Style all essentially tied. Capability probes ask the model to do something within its known role (e.g. reflect, ask a clarifying question, hold a boundary), and the rubric rewards genuine register-appropriate engagement. The mechanism-level interpretation is that QPM's higher purity proxy and its order-effect-sensitive trait state produce *slightly* more discriminating personality JSON over a 40-turn conversation, and that discrimination shows up most where the rubric measures register-appropriate competence. The effect is too small to reach significance at n = 960, but the direction (QPM > CMG on C-dim) is consistent with the mechanism.

T, E, S are not affected because:

- **T** is near-ceiling for both models (4.91 / 4.91) — Aria's trait register is already locked in by the fixed system prompt regardless of personality JSON.
- **E** is near-floor for both models (3.22 / 3.19) — episodic recall is gated by the RAG infrastructure (which is shared), not by the personality JSON.
- **S** is near-ceiling for both models (4.95 / 4.94) — style is locked in by the LoRA-10K adapter trained on Aria-shaped responses.

This pattern argues that the QPM advantage, where it exists at all in downstream behaviour, is concentrated in dimensions where the LLM has *headroom* to react to subtle personality-JSON differences — i.e. mid-band capability behaviour rather than near-ceiling Trait/Style or floor-bound Episodic.

Both results confirm the QPM internal-state advantage is mechanism-level, not an artefact of the specific psychotherapy parameter calibration.

## 6. Limitations

1. **H3 not detected, not refuted.** The 95% CI on the per-probe Δ includes zero; the point estimate is +0.024. A larger n (~3,000+ paired probes) could detect a d_z = 0.05 effect at 80% power. The pre-registered n = 960 was sized for d_z ≥ ~0.2 (conventional "small"), so the experiment is not properly powered for the effect size actually present. A follow-up with n = 3,000+ would either confirm a real-but-tiny QPM behavioural advantage or definitively refute it.
2. **Single SLM and adapter.** Battery C uses Qwen2.5-7B + LoRA-10K only. The QPM→JSON→LLM channel may have different sensitivity at different model scales (e.g. 14B+ may pick up subtler personality-JSON differences) or under different adapters (a smaller LoRA may leave more headroom for personality JSON to matter).
3. **JSON-mediated personality interface.** The QPM→structured-intent translation discards the off-diagonal coherence/purity information. A logits-level interface (e.g. using the QPM density matrix to steer SLM logits directly) is the natural next experiment but is out of scope here.
4. **Single domain for Battery C.** The 30 scripts are all psychotherapy scenarios from the Exp 1 bank. Software_eng was a robustness check on H1/H2, not a domain test for H3. A genuine cross-domain H3 test would require an SE-flavoured script bank.
5. **CMG-CDK is one classical baseline.** It is matched to QPM on every dimension except quantum-specific mechanisms, which is the strongest classical control for the specific claims being tested. However, it is not the *only* possible classical baseline (e.g. a context-dependent kernel with a non-additive update could in principle reproduce some order effects). The decision rule explicitly notes this: the H1 result rules out the strongest reasonable classical alternative, not literally all classical models.
6. **Judge stochasticity.** Sonnet 4.5 inter-replica scoring noise is non-trivial (Exp 1 §10.7.7 reported κ_w = 0.611 for intra-judge agreement). The H3 CI of ±0.048 absorbs some of this; the H1 and H2 effects are far above this noise floor.
7. **No SLM-side replication.** Battery C was run once per profile. The QPM-vs-CMG comparison is paired within (script, turn, dimension), but the SLM generation step is itself stochastic (temperature = 0.7) — a second run with different SLM seeds is not available to estimate within-experiment SLM-stochastic variance. This is a known cost of A100 budget management.

## 7. Recommendations for Future Work

1. **Larger-n re-run of Battery C.** Push to n = 3,000+ paired probes (5–10× current) to either detect the small Capability-dimension QPM advantage at p < 0.05 or definitively close the H3 question. Cost is linear in n; ~$50–80 in additional API + GPU.
2. **Logits-level QPM→SLM interface.** Replace the JSON-mediated `qpm_to_structured_intent` with a direct steering interface — e.g. condition SLM token logits on the full QPM density matrix or on purity/coherence proxies. This tests whether the H3 null is an interface artefact or a fundamental result.
3. **Multi-scale subject models.** Repeat Battery C on Qwen2.5-14B + LoRA and on a 3B-scale model. If H3 reaches significance at 3B (where the SLM has less "lock-in" to its persona) but not at 7B/14B, that would localise the QPM behavioural advantage to specific model-scale regimes.
4. **Cross-domain Battery C.** Generate a software-engineering script bank (30 scripts, equivalent rubric) and re-run Battery C on software_eng profile in its native domain. This would test whether the QPM advantage is domain-locked or profile-locked.
5. **Battery D — joint H1+H2 stress test.** Combine the order-effect inputs and the conflict scenarios into a single multi-turn sequence and measure whether the QPM signature persists across both stress conditions simultaneously.
6. **Replace decoherence parameters with measured values.** The current Table-4 decoherence values are calibration-derived defaults; if the QPM is to be claimed as physically grounded, the noise model parameters should be either ablated (sensitivity analysis: does H1/H2 hold across γ, λ ranges?) or sourced from a documented physical analogue.

## 10. Conclusion

Experiment 3 ablates the Quantum Personality Model against a deliberately matched classical baseline (CMG-CDK) that shares the same correlation structure, the same context-coupling δ, and the same s_k initialisation — but lacks superposition, coherence, and non-commutative evolution. The pre-registered decision rule fires the **H1✓ H2✓ H3✗** branch:

- **H1 (order effects)** is confirmed with Cohen's d = **21.51** (p ≈ 10⁻⁴⁰). QPM accumulates ~0.22 bits of non-commutative order-effect asymmetry per pair across all five input categories and both personality profiles; CMG-CDK is identically zero by the commutative-additive structure of its update rule.
- **H2 (ambivalence)** is confirmed with Cohen's d = **2.59** (p ≈ 10⁻¹⁰). QPM holds higher-entropy, lower-purity states under conflict than CMG-CDK can represent, replicating across both profiles with the effect slightly stronger on software_eng.
- **H3 (downstream PersonaScore)** is **not detected** at n = 960 paired probes. The observed QPM−CMG delta is +0.024 (d_z = 0.032, p = 0.327), well below the conventional small-effect threshold (0.2). The 95% CI includes zero. A small Capability-dimension lead (+0.059) is consistent with the QPM internal-state mechanism but does not reach significance at this sample size.

The H4 variance calibration passed with SNR = 60.23 (vs 3.0 threshold), confirming that shot noise does not dominate any of the comparisons.

**Bottom line:** The QPM's quantum-like advantage over the matched classical baseline is confirmed at the internal-personality-state level with extraordinarily large effect sizes, but is not detected at the downstream-behavioural level at the sample size pre-registered for this experiment. The CA v3 paper's claims at §2.3 are calibrated to this finding: internal-state quantum mechanisms are empirically grounded; downstream behavioural advantage is reframed as not detected at current sample size rather than asserted. The JSON-mediated QPM→SLM interface is the most likely site of the lost signal, and a logits-level interface is the highest-priority follow-up experiment.
